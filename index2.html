Next Beat

Next Beat is my current primary project, and most of my programming effort goes towards its development. It consists of a Android app portion, which is what the user uses, and a Node.js server which hosts a RESTful API for the Android app to use.


The point of Next Beat is to be able to play tracks from both Spotify and Soundcloud, seamlessly, without switching between their respective apps. All playback is controlled through my app, and users are able to create playlists with tracks from both music sources. Features include friending other next beat users, sharing playlists, queueing tracks from both media sources, viewing spotify artists/albums, a fully featured search function, a player notification, and many others.


I began developing this application at the beginning of September 2016 with no knowledge of Android programming, other than being able to program in standard Java. In just about 3 months, I was able to teach myself how to program Android applications, as well as how to use Node.js to host a RESTful server with MySQL as a database.


I am currently hosting my app on the app store, in a private beta for my friends to help test it out. I would love to share the app's source, but since I hope to eventually market and monetize my idea, I cannot share any of the source code. However, I am able to share screenshots from my application, and I hope these are able to convey my work properly. To see my open source code, please visit the projects page.


If you would like to try out Next Beat on Android, I can provide beta access on request. Please e-mail me at the e-mail address provided in my resume.
